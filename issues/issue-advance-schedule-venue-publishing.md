## Issue
USAB does not publish a confirmed full-season schedule with venue details far enough in advance for families to plan travel and logistics for major events. Late or incomplete announcements — particularly for JSE and Junior Nationals — force families into last-minute bookings at higher cost, create unnecessary school and work scheduling conflicts, and disproportionately disadvantage families with less flexibility to absorb short notice.

**Tags**: `transparency` `scheduling` `junior` `operations` `community` `JSE` `JN`

## Context
Junior badminton at the national level requires significant family commitment: cross-country travel, hotel stays, time off school and work, and coordination across multiple household schedules. The viability of that commitment depends heavily on how far in advance families know where and when they need to be.

The current pattern of late announcements creates compounding problems:

- **Cost**: Flight and hotel prices increase substantially within 60–90 days of travel. Families who book early pay significantly less than those forced to book late due to delayed announcements.
- **School and work scheduling**: Many parents need weeks or months to arrange leave, and students may need to coordinate with schools around absences. Late announcements shrink that window or eliminate it entirely.
- **Venue matters independently of dates**: Knowing the date is insufficient without a city — a JN in California vs. one in the Midwest is an entirely different travel plan and cost profile. Partial information forces families to either over-plan (booking refundable, more expensive options) or gamble.
- **Inequity**: Families with greater financial flexibility or remote-work arrangements absorb late notice more easily. Those with tighter budgets or rigid schedules are systematically disadvantaged by late publishing — making national-level participation a function of logistical advantage, not athletic merit.
- **Compounding effect of changes**: When dates or venues shift after an initial announcement (a recurring concern raised in the governance transparency issue), families who planned around the first announcement bear additional cost and disruption. Earlier, more stable announcements reduce the frequency and impact of such changes.

Comparable youth sports organizations (USAV volleyball, USTA junior tennis, US Youth Soccer) routinely publish full-season national event calendars 6–12 months in advance. USAB's current timeline falls short of that standard.

## Potential Options

> The options below are illustrative examples only and are not necessarily the best or final solutions. Better options may come from the community, clubs, or USAB directly.

- **Option A — Publish a full confirmed season calendar by a fixed annual deadline**: Commit to publishing all major event dates and host cities (JSE, JN, Adult Nationals, ORC) by a specific date each year — e.g., September 1 for the upcoming season. Requires completing the venue selection and scheduling process upstream of that deadline, but gives families a full planning horizon.

- **Option B — Two-phase announcement with guaranteed minimums**: Phase 1 (6+ months out): publish confirmed dates and host city/region. Phase 2 (3+ months out): publish full venue details, registration logistics, and hotel block information. Decouples date confirmation from full logistics, allowing earlier planning even if venue specifics are still being finalized.

- **Option C — Establish tiered minimum advance notice standards by event level**: Define and publish minimum lead times for each event tier — e.g., Tier 1 (JSE, JN): dates confirmed 6 months out, venue confirmed 4 months out; Tier 2 (ORC, regional): dates 3 months out, venue 2 months out. Creates accountability and sets clear expectations for host clubs and USAB staff.

- **Option D — Dedicated public season calendar page with change notifications**: Maintain a living, publicly accessible season calendar on the USAB website showing event status (tentative / confirmed / changed), with email or push notifications when details are updated or changed. Reduces the burden on families to actively check for updates and creates a visible record if announcements are consistently late.

## Open Questions

- What is the current typical lead time between announcement and event date for JSE and JN? Is this tracked?
- What internal processes — venue bidding cycles, board approvals, scheduling dependencies — currently prevent earlier announcements? Which of these can be accelerated?
- Is the venue selection process (addressed in the venue transparency issue) a bottleneck for schedule publication, and would resolving that upstream issue unlock earlier announcements?
- Do USAB bylaws or event hosting agreements specify any minimum advance notice obligations?
- What comparable national governing bodies publish multi-month advance season calendars, and what processes enable them to do so?
- Has USAB received formal feedback from families quantifying the impact of late announcements — e.g., cost estimates, withdrawal rates due to scheduling conflicts?
- Would a two-phase announcement model (dates before venue) be acceptable to families, or is the city/region information essential to the first announcement?
