## Issue
The USAB official website lacks dedicated pages for community awards, historical team records, and international competition links — missing opportunities to celebrate player achievements, inform families about nomination processes, and preserve institutional memory.

**Tags**: `community` `transparency` `junior` `high-performance`

## Context
The USAB website serves as the primary public-facing resource for the badminton community, yet several categories of information that matter to families, players, and clubs are either absent or difficult to find:

- **Award history is not centralized.** The Debbie Brown Award (volunteer/service recognition) and the annual athlete award lack dedicated pages listing past winners, nomination criteria, and voting timelines. With Junior Nationals approaching each year, families and clubs are left guessing about how to nominate deserving candidates and when deadlines fall.
- **International competition representation is invisible.** Players who qualify for the Pan American Junior Championships effectively represent Team USA Junior, yet there is no page listing these rosters by year. For junior players, making this team is a major milestone — and for recruits, having a publicly accessible record supports college recruitment visibility.
- **Pan American Championships links are missing.** There is no easy way to find results or information about past and current Pan Am Championships from the USAB site, despite these being among the most significant international events for US players.

These gaps affect junior players and their families most directly, but also reduce the visibility of the sport and the community's ability to celebrate its own achievements.

## Potential Options

> The options below are illustrative examples only and are not necessarily the best or final solutions. Better options may come from the community, clubs, players, or USAB directly. Please add your suggestions via pull request or discussion.

- **Option A — Debbie Brown Award page**: Create a dedicated page listing all past winners, the nomination criteria, voting process, and annual timeline (ideally with deadlines published well before JN). This makes the process accessible to the entire community rather than only those with insider knowledge.
- **Option B — Annual athlete award page**: Create a companion page for the yearly athlete award with the same structure — past winners, eligibility criteria, and nomination/voting process. If both awards share a similar cycle, a single "USAB Awards" section with sub-pages could work.
- **Option C — Pan Am Championships links**: Add a page or section linking to past and current Pan American Championships results, schedules, and any USAB-related coverage. This connects the domestic community to the international stage.
- **Option D — Pan Am Junior team rosters by year**: Publish a historical record of players who qualified for the Pan American Junior Championships each year. This serves as a de facto "Team USA Junior" archive — valuable for player recognition, college recruitment portfolios, and community pride.

## Open Questions

- What is the official name of the annual athlete award, and is the selection process documented anywhere currently?
- Does USAB have historical records of Pan Am Junior qualifiers readily available, or would this require community-sourced data?
- Is there a technical or staffing constraint preventing new pages from being added to the current website, or is this primarily a prioritization gap?
- Could these pages be maintained collaboratively (e.g., community volunteers updating rosters) to reduce the burden on USAB staff?
