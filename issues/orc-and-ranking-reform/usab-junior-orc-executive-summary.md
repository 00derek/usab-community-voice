# USAB Junior ORC Format and Ranking Reform - Executive Summary

## 1. Recommendation

USAB should approve a coupled format and ranking change for junior ORC events, beginning with a single-region, single-event pilot in the next applicable season. The change caps crunch events at R64 with a parallel B-flight running the same weekend, drops the redundant CRC tier, and bumps OLC from 15% to 20% of JN. ORC's headline 50% weight is preserved. The full design is in the linked spec; this summary supports a go/no-go decision on opening Phase 1.

## 2. Problem

Multi-event players in the five crunch events (BS U13/U15/U17, XD U13/U15) routinely face 7-9 matches in a single day at ORC, because R128 single-elimination forces 7 rounds into a 3-day weekend. The structural cause is the 50%-vs-15% gap between ORC and OLC/CRC: it concentrates ranking-conscious players at ORC events regardless of distance or readiness, swelling fields beyond what the format can absorb.

## 3. Proposed Changes

| Element             | Current              | Proposed                                                                                                        |
| ------------------- | -------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Crunch-event format | R128 single bracket  | R64 A-flight + R64 B-flight, parallel, same weekend                                                             |
| ORC weight          | 50% of JN            | 50% of JN (unchanged)                                                                                           |
| ORC B-flight weight | n/a                  | 15% of JN (= 30% of A-flight)                                                                                   |
| OLC weight          | 15% of JN            | 20% of JN                                                                                                       |
| CRC weight          | 15% of JN            | Dropped (hosts may convert to OLC)                                                                              |
| A-flight entry      | n/a (single bracket) | Top 64 by national ranking. Edge cases (BWF, playing-up) deferred to a separate workstream — see Open Questions |
| Counting            | Best-4 sum           | Best-4 sum (unchanged)                                                                                          |
| Tournament length   | 3 days               | 3 days (no Friday extension)                                                                                    |

## 4. Why This Works

- **Operationally near-neutral on venue load.** Total matches: 128 each (including 3rd/4th place playoffs). Per-round simultaneous match counts are identical for rounds 1-6. Per-day match counts at the venue are essentially the same. Hosts do not need more courts, more officials, or a 4th day.
- **Per-player welfare improves materially.** Six rounds in three days (vs seven) eliminates the forced 3-rounds-in-one-day compression on Sunday or Monday. For deep-run multi-event players, this is roughly 2-3 fewer matches on the worst day.
- **OLC bump preserves the tier hierarchy.** 2 x OLC gold = 80% of 1 x ORC gold, so the "2 OLC < 1 ORC" bound holds with margin. 3 x OLC gold beats 1 x ORC gold by only 20%, which is the intended modest reward for sustained activity under best-4.
- **The crowding pressure is relieved structurally, not by devaluing ORC.** B-flight at 15% of JN combined with OLC at 20% means a #80-ranked player has both a higher ceiling and higher floor staying local for OLC than traveling to land in B-flight. ORC's 50% is preserved for top players who earn it on merit.
- **No 4-day tournament.** A Friday qualifier was considered and explicitly rejected: it would create de facto 4-day school/work absence and increase host operating cost.
- **Counting model unchanged.** Best-4 sum is retained as-is. The "best-4 overweights peaks" concern is partially addressed by the tier-weight compression (smaller marginal benefit from any single high-tier result) and by the B-flight introduction (players locked-in on best-4 still have incentive to compete because B-flight at later events offers meaningful points). Further counting-model adjustments are deferred and can be revisited if these changes prove insufficient.
- **Edge cases (BWF, playing-up) are not solved by this proposal.** A wildcard mechanism was considered and rejected as adding governance complexity without clearly outperforming a more principled approach. These cases are real and acknowledged but are scoped to a separate workstream — see Open Questions.

## 5. What's Changing for Whom

| Stakeholder                  | Change                                                                                                                                                 | Net                                     |
| ---------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | --------------------------------------- |
| Top 64 by ranking            | A-flight is similar to today; one fewer round; better daily distribution                                                                               | Positive                                |
| Mid-ranked (#65-128)         | New B-flight pathway with meaningful points; OLC more attractive locally                                                                               | Positive                                |
| Lower-ranked / new players   | OLC now worth 20%; B-flight available if traveling                                                                                                     | Slightly positive                       |
| Playing-up / BWF-experienced | Not directly addressed; lands in B-flight if domestic ranking is outside top 64. Acknowledged gap, deferred to a separate edge-case seeding workstream | Neutral (no improvement, no regression) |
| Host clubs                   | Two brackets to manage; total court-hours and officiating-hours unchanged; one extra final on Monday                                                   | Neutral; manageable                     |
| Officials                    | Reduced standard for B-flight early rounds (roving umpire / self-report); full officiating from B-flight QF onward                                     | Neutral                                 |
| CRC hosts                    | Convert to OLC or run as non-ranking developmental                                                                                                     | Neutral; preserves choice               |

## 6. Worked Example

BS U15 at South ORC with 100 registrations: top 64 enter A-flight directly by national ranking; the remaining 36 enter B-flight (R64 with byes). An A-flight winner plays 6 matches over 3 days (2/day). Today, the same player would play 7 over 3 days with at least one 3-match day.

## 7. Phased Rollout

**Phase 1**: Pilot in one region with one or two crunch events (suggested: BS U15 in the highest-entry region) in the next applicable season. Apply tier reweighting and A/B-flight format for that pilot. Document operational and player-experience outcomes.

**Phase 2**: Conditional on Phase 1 results, expand to all five crunch events nationally and apply tier reweighting nationally. Begin scoping a quality-of-field multiplier as a longer-term direction. Open a separate workstream on edge-case seeding inputs (BWF tour ranking points, playing-up cross-age-group ranking).

## 9. Open Questions for USAB

1. **Approval authority.** Which body has authority to approve tier reweighting and format changes - HPC, TOAG, board, or regional directors acting jointly? The proposal needs an unambiguous approval path.
2. **Vendor capability.** Does the current registration and seeding software support parallel brackets and B-flight points calculation? If not, what is the lead time and cost to add it?
3. **Pilot timing.** When exactly does Phase 1 land in the existing season calendar, and which region is best positioned (highest historical entry counts in BS U15 and host capacity to run the pilot)?
4. **Edge-case seeding inputs (BWF tour ranking).** A-flight entry under this proposal is purely top 64 by domestic ranking, which leaves BWF-experienced players with thin domestic records in B-flight. This is a real gap. The proposal scopes a fix to a separate workstream because a wildcard mechanism was considered and judged to add governance complexity without clearly outperforming a more principled approach (e.g., defining how BWF tour ranking points should be incorporated as a seeding input, or a quality-of-field multiplier in Phase 2). USAB needs to decide whether to charter that workstream and on what timeline.
5. **Edge-case seeding inputs (playing-up).** Same status as item 4. Players competing up an age group hold understated rankings in the older division. The proposal does not solve this; a separate workstream would need to define how cross-age-group ranking is combined with same-age ranking for playing-up players.
6. **Crunch designation rule.** Fixed list of five events, or annual recalibration based on prior-year entry data?
7. **Should USAB drop or reduce its 30% revenue cut on OLC events?** The OLC weight bump (15% → 20%) is intended to redistribute ranking-conscious participation from saturated ORCs to locally-run OLCs. That redistribution only fully works if the supply of OLC events grows to match new demand, which depends on host economics. Reducing or removing USAB's 30% cut on OLC specifically would let hosts share in the participation upside, making OLC more attractive to run — aligning player-side and host-side incentives. Without this lever, the reform improves OLC's attractiveness to players but leaves host economics unchanged. Sits inside the broader financial-sustainability concern in [`../issue-financial-sustainability-membership-hosting-officiating.md`](../issue-financial-sustainability-membership-hosting-officiating.md).
