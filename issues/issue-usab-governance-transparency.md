## Issue
USAB makes significant decisions affecting the community — tournament schedule changes, board composition, and governance processes — without adequate public communication, documented rationale, or meaningful stakeholder input. The absence of a standing forum for community engagement means concerns accumulate without resolution, and trust erodes each time a major decision lands without explanation.

**Tags**: `transparency` `governance` `community` `board` `elections` `accountability`

## Context
Several recent and recurring examples illustrate the pattern:

- **JN date shifts**: Changes to Junior Nationals dates from usual 4th July weekend affect schedules, family travel planning, and coach availability. When dates shift without advance notice or public rationale, families bear the cost of last-minute adjustments with no understanding of why the change was made or who was consulted.

- **Election and reelection process**: How board members are elected or reelected is not clearly communicated. Many community members — including active club leaders and parents — are unaware of when elections occur, who is eligible to vote, how candidates are nominated, or how to participate. Low awareness suppresses participation and concentrates governance in a small group.

Taken together, these gaps create a governance environment where decisions appear to come from a black box. Even well-reasoned decisions lose legitimacy when the community has no window into how they were made. The issues filed in this repo — on venue selection, medical staffing, ranking delays, and retirement rules — share a common root: policies and decisions made without structured community input or transparent communication.

## Potential Options

> The options below are illustrative examples only and are not necessarily the best or final solutions. Better options may come from the community, clubs, or USAB directly.

- **Option A — Hold regular public town halls**: Schedule quarterly or semi-annual town halls (in-person or virtual) where USAB leadership presents key upcoming decisions, reports on recent actions, and takes live questions from the community. Provides a direct, interactive forum and signals that leadership is accessible. Recordings should be published for those who cannot attend.

- **Option B — Establish a public community forum**: Create an official, moderated online platform (e.g., a community forum, Discord server, or dedicated section of the USAB website) where members can raise issues, ask questions, and discuss proposals. Decentralizes communication beyond town halls and creates a searchable record of community concerns and USAB responses.

- **Option C — Publish board meeting minutes and decision rationale**: After each board meeting, publish a summary of decisions made, motions passed, and the reasoning behind significant choices. Does not require sharing all internal deliberations — but key decisions affecting the community (schedule changes, policy approvals, budget allocations) should have a public record.

- **Option D — Publish a transparent board roster with tenure and election timeline**: Maintain a publicly accessible page showing current board members, their roles, term start and end dates, and the upcoming election calendar. Publish candidate profiles and voting procedures well in advance of elections, and clearly communicate eligibility requirements for voting and nomination.

- **Option E — Establish a community advisory panel with formal input rights**: Create a standing advisory panel drawn from clubs, parents, coaches, and athletes that is consulted before major decisions (schedule changes, rule changes, policy approvals). Panel input does not bind the board, but must be acknowledged in any published decision rationale. Formalizes the community's voice without requiring a governance overhaul.

## Open Questions

- Which categories of decisions are currently communicated publicly, and which are handled internally without public notice?
- Are USAB board meeting minutes recorded and, if so, are they accessible to members?
- What is the current board election process — who is eligible to vote, how are candidates nominated, and how are elections announced?
- Are there existing term limits or tenure caps for board members? If so, are they enforced and publicly documented?
- What prompted the most recent JN date shift, and was any community or stakeholder input sought before the change was finalized?
- Has USAB conducted any prior town halls or community forums? If so, what was the format and what did or did not work?
- Are there USAB bylaws or governing documents that define transparency and communication obligations, and are they publicly available?
- What is the minimum viable first step — is there appetite within the board for any of these options, and who would champion implementation?
