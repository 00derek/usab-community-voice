# USAB Community Issues

This repository documents community concerns about USAB governance, junior player welfare, and tournament operations. Each issue is written to be constructive — capturing context, tradeoffs, and open questions rather than just complaints — with the goal of informing productive dialogue between the community and USAB.

Issues are organized into three areas: **Governance & Transparency**, **Player Welfare & Competition**, and **Tournament Operations & Format**.

---

## Governance & Transparency

### [USAB Governance Transparency](issues/issue-usab-governance-transparency.md)
Key USAB decisions — including Junior Nationals date shifts, board tenure, and the election and reelection process — are made without adequate public communication or stakeholder input. The community has no standing forum to ask questions or raise concerns, and decisions that significantly affect families arrive without explanation. This issue examines options ranging from regular town halls and a public community forum to published board meeting minutes and a transparent election calendar.

### [Communication Responsiveness](issues/issue-communication-responsiveness.md)
Emails from parents and clubs frequently go unanswered, and critical information reaches some community members through informal channels while others remain unaware. The result is a two-tier community where access to information depends on personal connections rather than official channels. This issue covers both the reactive problem (no feedback loop on inquiries) and the proactive problem (information asymmetry), and explores options including a formal inquiry system with published SLAs, a designated community liaison, and a regular newsletter.

### [Advance Schedule and Venue Publishing](issues/issue-advance-schedule-venue-publishing.md)
USAB does not publish confirmed event dates and venue details far enough in advance for families to plan travel and logistics. Late announcements for JSE and Junior Nationals force expensive last-minute bookings and disproportionately disadvantage families with less scheduling flexibility. This issue explores minimum advance notice standards, a two-phase announcement model (dates before venue details), and a public season calendar with change notifications.

### [Junior Event and Venue Selection Transparency](issues/issue-junior-event-venue-selection-transparency.md)
With 5–6 clubs bidding for JSE hosting rights and no published selection criteria, the community has no visibility into how hosting decisions are made for JSE or Junior Nationals. Clubs that lose bids receive no feedback, and the perception of opaque decision-making erodes trust regardless of whether decisions themselves are sound. Options include publishing a formal bid scoring rubric, establishing a selection committee with community representation, and requiring post-decision rationale statements.

## Player Welfare & Competition

### [On-Site Medical and Physiotherapy Standards](issues/issue-on-site-medical-physio-standards.md)
Regional club events like NorCal OLC have on-site physios, but JSE and Junior Nationals do not — signaling the absence of a top-down standard from USAB. Without qualified medical staff, injured junior players have no timely assessment, face pressure to continue playing, and leave USAB and host clubs with unaddressed liability exposure. This issue explores mandatory medical staffing requirements, a national sports medicine sponsorship, and university partnership models to make coverage sustainable.

### [JSE Retirement Rule — Unintended Consequences](issues/issue-jse-retirement-rule-unintended-consequences.md)
A 2025 retirement penalty rule — where withdrawing from one event triggers consequences in other events — was approved by the High Performance Committee and applied to JSE, producing the opposite of its intended effect: rather than retiring, players played deliberately passive consolation matches, wasting court time and opponent effort. This issue examines options including suspending the rule at junior events pending review, distinguishing between main draw and consolation round obligations, and establishing a multi-stakeholder policy review process before future rule changes are extended.

### [Selection Format and Ranking Points System](issues/issue-selection-format-and-ranking-points-system.md)
The junior selection system has two compounding flaws: single-elimination draws mean draw luck significantly influences who advances, and a ranking structure counting only the best 4 events with large tier differentials (JN >> ORC >> OLC/CRC) means points standings may not reflect true player quality entering selection. Additional blind spots exist in the seeding model — players competing in older age groups and those with BWF international experience but no domestic junior points can eliminate top seeds in early rounds. Options explored include seeding formula refinements, a capped round-robin format, BWF points integration, ranking tier rebalancing, and a two-stage regional pre-qualifier feeding into a national selection event.

### [Junior Ranking Update Delays](issues/issue-junior-ranking-update-delays.md)
USAB has committed to monthly junior ranking updates, but recurring delays mean families make tournament entry and travel decisions against stale data — affecting seeding, JSE/JN selection eligibility, and college recruitment visibility. The repeated failure to meet a stated commitment points to a structural process problem, not an isolated incident. This issue maps options from auditing and documenting the current process, automating result ingestion from tournament software, assigning dedicated ownership with a published update calendar, to building a public ranking status dashboard.

## Tournament Operations & Format

### [ORC Format — Match Load and Tournament Length](issues/issue-orc-format-match-load-and-tournament-length.md)
The ORC's 3-day format starting from a round of 128 results in some players scheduled for 7–9 matches in a single day — creating injury risk, long delays, and non-competitive conditions. The problem is structural: a ranking points differential that makes ORC significantly more valuable than OLC/CRC inflates field sizes beyond what the format can support. Options include entry caps with qualification thresholds, extending to a 4-day format, splitting ORC into tiered divisions, and rebalancing ranking points across event tiers — each with real tradeoffs for families, host clubs, officials, and volunteers.

### [Financial Sustainability — Membership, Hosting, and Officiating](issues/issue-financial-sustainability-membership-hosting-officiating.md)
The financial model underpinning USAB-sanctioned badminton is under strain across multiple groups: families face rising membership costs without visible service improvements; host clubs absorb a 30% USAB revenue cut, with JSE hosting adding a ~$20,000 bidding commitment that reduces the club's net revenue share and effectively excludes smaller clubs from bidding; and umpires receive a $40/day stipend that does not cover their actual costs of travel, membership, and Safe Sport certification. Without a corporate sponsorship strategy, all of these costs stay within the community. Options include a transparent financial review, a revised revenue sharing model, improved official compensation, and a dedicated corporate sponsorship program.
