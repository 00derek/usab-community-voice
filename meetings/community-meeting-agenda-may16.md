## 5. ORC Format & Ranking Reform Proposal (15 min)

The ORC format and ranking system have two coupled problems. First, a format problem: the current R128 single-elimination draw at crunch events (BS U13/U15/U17, XD U13/U15) compresses 7 rounds into 3 days, producing 7–9 matches per day for multi-event players — an attrition contest that results in injuries and withdrawals. Second, a ranking problem: ORC is worth 50% of JN while OLC and CRC are worth only 15%, creating intense pressure for every ranking-conscious player to travel to ORC regardless of distance or readiness, which is why ORC fields swell to ~700 entries and the format breaks down.

A community-drafted proposal addresses both together: cap crunch events at R64 with a parallel B-flight (no 4th day needed), drop the CRC tier, bump OLC from 15% to 20% of JN, keep ORC A-flight at 50%, and keep the best-4 sum counting model unchanged. Phase 1 is a single-region, single-event pilot. The full spec and concerns document are in the repository.

- Has USAB or HPC had a chance to review the proposal?
- Any concerns or objections the community should address before it goes to HPC?
- Four open questions that need USAB input before Phase 1 can move:
  - Which body has authority to approve tier reweighting and format changes (HPC, TOAG, board)?
  - Does current registration/seeding software support parallel dual brackets?
  - Which region and event is best positioned for the pilot, and what season does Phase 1 land in?
  - Should the edge-case seeding workstream (BWF tour ranking, playing-up) start in parallel with Phase 1 or after?

**Proposed next action**: HPC or TOAG makes a go/no-go decision on opening Phase 1, with a target date for that decision.
